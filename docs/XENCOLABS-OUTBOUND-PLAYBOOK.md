## ANGLE 2: "Your form kills conversion"

**Use when:** The company has an 8+ field contact form, no self-assessment or quiz funnel, and a single generic CTA for all visitor types. Very common with companies that use HubSpot or Salesforce web forms out of the box.

### Email 2A — The math angle

**Subject:** 8 fields × 4-6% drop per field

I looked at [Company]'s contact page. You're asking for [list 3-4 of their actual fields: name, company, title, email, phone, industry, country, message].

Every form field you add drops conversion by 4-6%. At 8+ fields, you're filtering out a significant percentage of interested buyers before they ever talk to your sales team.

The fix isn't removing fields — it's replacing the form with something that captures more useful data in fewer steps. Three questions that tell your sales team the prospect's size, current situation, and budget sensitivity — then just name, email, company to close.

Your sales team gets better leads with more context. The prospect gets a faster experience. Both sides win.

This is one of the first things we fix in our growth engagements. I can show you exactly how it works.

## HOW TO PERSONALIZE

Every email above has brackets like [Company], [keyword], [competitor]. These must be filled in with real, specific observations. Here's how to research each prospect in 10 minutes:

1. Visit their homepage. Write down their hero headline verbatim. Note if it's feature-first or pain-first.
2. Count their contact form fields.
3. Check if they have different landing pages for different buyer types, or just one generic path.
4. Visit their blog. Count the posts. Read 3-5 titles. Note if they're editorial ("Thoughts on our industry") or search-targeted ("How to choose a [product] provider").
5. Google "[their product category] + [their main keyword]." See if they rank on page 1. Note who does.
6. Check one competitor's site for comparison.

That gives you enough for a specific, credible email. Do not send generic emails. Every prospect should feel like you actually looked at their site — because you did.

## SEQUENCE CADENCE

For each prospect:

**Day 1:** Send Email 1 (whichever angle fits best)
**Day 4:** Send Follow-up if no reply
**Day 8:** Send Email from a different angle (if the first didn't land, try a different pain point)
**Day 12:** Final follow-up — short, 2 sentences: "Last note on this. If the timing isn't right, no worries. But if you want to see what I'd fix first on [Company]'s site, I'm happy to walk through it. — Xen"

If no reply after 4 touches, move on. Do not spam. Quality over volume.

## PROSPECT IDENTIFICATION CRITERIA

### Ideal company profile:

Revenue: $5M – $100M
Employees: 20 – 500
Geography: U.S. (start here, expand later)
B2B: Must be selling to other businesses, not consumers

### Red flags to skip:
- Venture-backed startups with in-house growth teams (they don't need you)
- Companies with excellent sites already (nothing to fix)
- Businesses under $3M revenue (can't afford the engagement)
- Companies currently in an agency relationship (hard to displace, messy)

### Green flags that indicate fit:
- Website hero is feature-first, not pain-first
- Contact form has 6+ fields
- Blog exists but titles are editorial, not keyword-targeted
- No visible quiz funnel, self-assessment, or interactive content
- Same CTA for all visitor types
- Domain was registered 5+ years ago (established business, not a startup)
- LinkedIn shows sales team but no marketing or growth leader
- Competitors have better sites

### Best verticals to start (you already have proof in these):

**1. Cybersecurity / IT / Managed Services**
You literally built NexusGuard's demo site. This is your strongest proof vertical.

**2. ITAD / Electronics Recycling / Data Destruction**
AER Worldwide is your proof. The Morgan Stanley $163M angle works for every company in this space.

**3. Manufacturing / Industrial B2B**
These companies have 30+ year track records, real products, real revenue, and websites that look like they were built in 2014.

**4. Professional Services (Law, Accounting, Consulting)**
High client lifetime value, terrible websites, and they understand the value of inbound leads because their entire business depends on them.

**5. Regional Service Businesses / Multi-Location**
CM Creative is your proof. The "zero upfront, revenue share" model works perfectly here.

## DAILY ROUTINE (14 days)

**Every morning, 90 minutes:**
1. Research 5 new prospects (10 min each = 50 min)
2. Write and send 5 personalized emails (8 min each = 40 min)

**Every afternoon, 30 minutes:**
3. Follow up on previous emails
4. Reply to any responses
5. Book calls

**Target by day 14:**
- 50 prospects researched and emailed
- 5-8 replies
- 3-5 calls booked
- 1-2 audit requests or sprint conversations

That's enough to validate the motion and know which angle to scale.
